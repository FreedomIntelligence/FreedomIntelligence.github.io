<p class="impact-lede">
Human-agent interaction is the connective tissue between model capability and real deployment. This project line studies agents that interact with people, learners, patients, investors, speech partners, and simulated worlds, where behavior unfolds over time rather than in a single static prompt.
</p>

<section class="impact-section">
  <h2>Background and Motivation</h2>
  <div class="impact-grid">
    <div class="impact-card">
      <strong>Static benchmarks miss interaction</strong>
      <p>Many failures only appear after several turns: users change goals, agents adapt, environments respond, and earlier decisions constrain later ones.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-card">
      <strong>Human-facing agents need social realism</strong>
      <p>Medical learners, investors, patients, and speech users bring beliefs, emotions, incentives, and incomplete information into the loop.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-card">
      <strong>Simulation creates safe testbeds</strong>
      <p>Before deploying agents in clinics, markets, classrooms, or social settings, we can study them in controlled and instrumented environments.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="impact-section">
  <h2>Research Storyline</h2>
  <div class="impact-storyline">
    <div class="impact-step">
      <span>User</span>
      <div><strong>Simulate users for dialogue learning</strong><p>LLM user simulators and Socratic questioning systems generate interactive training environments for multi-turn dialogue.</p></div>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-step">
      <span>Patient</span>
      <div><strong>Turn AI into a medical learning partner</strong><p>AI standardized patients support repeatable practice in history taking, communication, and clinical reasoning.</p></div>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-step">
      <span>Society</span>
      <div><strong>Study multi-agent social and market behavior</strong><p>TwinMarket and Economic World Models use agent societies to examine emergent markets, policy sandboxes, and collective risk.</p></div>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-step">
      <span>World</span>
      <div><strong>Build environments where state evolves</strong><p>MicroVerse extends interaction to scientific worlds, where agents must reason about hidden mechanisms and changing biological states.</p></div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="impact-section">
  <h2>Project Clusters</h2>
  <div class="impact-grid">
    <div class="impact-card">
      <strong>Dialogue and user simulation</strong>
      <p>Large Language Model as a User Simulator and PlatoLM study how simulated users can teach, probe, and improve dialogue models.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-card">
      <strong>Medical education agents</strong>
      <p>EasyMED and AI standardized patients connect human-agent interaction with medical training and learner-centered evaluation.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-card">
      <strong>Agentic markets and economies</strong>
      <p>TwinMarket and Economic World Models make multi-agent interaction observable at social, financial, and policy scale.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-card">
      <strong>Speech and embodied interaction</strong>
      <p>S2S-Arena, EchoMind, and speech-to-speech Turing tests examine whether agents can interact naturally through voice and paralinguistic signals.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
