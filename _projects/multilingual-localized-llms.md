<p class="impact-lede">
The multilingual model line makes open LLMs useful outside the English-centric default. Phoenix democratized chat models across languages, AceGPT localized LLMs for Arabic, and Apollo extends medical LLMs across many languages with models, corpora, and benchmarks.
</p>

<section class="impact-section">
  <h2>Research Storyline</h2>
  <div class="impact-storyline">
    <div class="impact-step">
      <span>General</span>
      <div><strong>Phoenix starts from multilingual chat access</strong><p>The early model line asks how open chat models can serve users across languages rather than only English-heavy settings.</p></div>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-step">
      <span>Localize</span>
      <div><strong>AceGPT focuses on Arabic alignment</strong><p>Arabic LLM localization shows that alignment data, vocabulary, culture, and evaluation must be designed for the target language.</p></div>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-step">
      <span>Medicine</span>
      <div><strong>Apollo moves localization into healthcare</strong><p>Apollo combines multilingual medical corpora, models, and benchmarks so medical AI can cover many more language communities.</p></div>
    </div>
    <div class="impact-step">
      <span>Scale</span>
      <div><strong>ApolloMoE makes language-family specialization efficient</strong><p>Mixture-of-experts style multilingual medical modeling keeps specialization while reducing the cost of serving many languages.</p></div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="impact-section">
  <h2>Why It Matters</h2>
  <ul class="impact-list">
    <li>Medical and public-interest AI cannot be accessible if it works only in a small set of high-resource languages.</li>
    <li>Localization is more than translation: model vocabulary, alignment data, evaluation, and cultural/domain assumptions all matter.</li>
    <li>The project connects general multilingual chat, Arabic-specific alignment, and multilingual medical AI into a single access agenda.</li>
  </ul>
</section>
